# Q4 - Data Structures, Algorithms and Object-Oriented Programming

By the end of this quarter you should be able to solve problems well-enough to pass Jr Developer whiteboard interviews and algorithm-based code challenges.

There are two main threads in this quarter:

- [Learning a technique for solving problems](https://github.com/gSchool/problem-solving-curriculum)
- [Learning data structures and algorithms](https://github.com/gSchool/computer-science-curriculum)

In this quarter you and your classmates will likely diverge significantly in the pace with which you move through data structures and algorithms, and so we introduce two new learning structures this quarter:

1. A group-based drill called "Solve It!", in which you learn how to solve problems at a whiteboard
1. More independent learning time in which you may not be working on the exact same exercises as your neighbor

Like all things, you learn to solve technical problems by _actually solving_ technical problems a lot.  So much of effective problem solving involves staying calm and falling back on your training when the going gets tough.  In order to get good at that, _the going must get tough_.  That means that there _should_ be times this quarter where you spend **up to a few hours wrestling with problems**.

In your career, the problems will keep getting harder, and the number of people who will have the solutions will decrease.  In this quarter you will get exposed to an authentic glimpse of what your day-to-day will look like as a software developer, and we provide the following scaffolding to make sure you learn as efficiently as possible:

- Well-honed sequencing.  There's a lot to cover.  We've tweaked the sequencing of exercises based on experience with hundreds of students
- Automated Tests.  At any point in the day you'll know if your code meets the specs or not, right away.
